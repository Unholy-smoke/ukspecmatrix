Group B: Design & Problem Solving
B1
Take an active role in the identification and... ▼ Take an active role in the identification and definition of project requirements, problems and opportunities.
Identify potential safety hazards early in the problem definition phase. Identify opportunities to reduce environmental impact before work begins (e.g. reuse). Identify ethical conflicts (e.g. dual use, harm to public) in the project scope. Ensure diverse stakeholders define the "problem" together (don't assume). Challenge whether the "problem" is the right one to solve.
B2
Can identify the appropriate investigations and... ▼ Can identify the appropriate investigations and research needed to undertake the design, development and analysis required to complete an engineering task and conduct these activities effectively.
Apply "Safety by Design" principles to eliminate risks. Select materials/methods that lower lifecycle carbon and protect nature. Refuse solutions that compromise integrity or public trust (even if cheaper). Design for diverse user needs (e.g. accessibility standards). Evaluate multiple design options against evidence.
B3
Can implement engineering tasks and evaluate... ▼ Can implement engineering tasks and evaluate the effectiveness of engineering solutions.
Implement safe systems of work during deployment/construction. Minimise waste, energy, and pollution during implementation. Report implementation issues/defects transparently; do not hide faults. Consider impact of construction/rollout on local communities. Analyse implementation data to check performance vs theory.
Group C: Management & Leadership
C1
Plan the work and resources needed to enable... ▼ Plan the work and resources needed to enable effective implementation of a significant engineering task or project.
Prioritise safety milestones in the project schedule. Include net-zero or biodiversity targets as project KPIs. Ensure contracts are fair; reject bribery/corruption in planning. Communicate plans clearly to all levels of the organisation. Anticipate project risks and plan contingencies.
C2
Manage (organise, direct and control), programme... ▼ Manage (organise, direct and control), programme or schedule, budget and resource elements of a significant engineering task or project.
Secure budget specifically for safety equipment/training. Allocate budget for sustainable alternatives (circular economy). Ensure supply chain transparency (modern slavery check). Ensure procurement supports diverse or local suppliers. Optimise resource usage to achieve maximum value.
C3
Lead teams or technical specialisms and assist... ▼ Lead teams or technical specialisms and assist others to meet changing technical and managerial needs.
Create a "psychologically safe" culture where staff can speak up. Train teams on climate literacy and sustainable practices. Treat staff fairly; uphold employment rights; handle grievances with integrity. Actively support EDI and diverse hiring practices. Mentor staff to develop their own analytical skills.
C4
Bring about continuous quality improvement and... ▼ Bring about continuous quality improvement and promote best practice.
Review safety incidents to prevent recurrence. Measure carbon/waste data to drive future environmental improvements. Adhere to codes of conduct; refuse to sign off substandard work. Seek feedback from diverse users to improve quality. Use feedback loops to refine processes systematically.
Group D: Communication (Competence)
D1
Communicate effectively with others, at all... ▼ Communicate effectively with others, at all levels, in English.
Write clear, unambiguous safety instructions or warnings. Report environmental data honestly (avoid "greenwashing"). Write reports that objectively present risks; do not manipulate data. Use plain English; avoid exclusionary jargon; use accessible formats. Structure written arguments logically to support conclusions.
D2
Clearly present and discuss proposals... ▼ Clearly present and discuss proposals, justifications and conclusions.
Deliver safety briefings clearly; ensure understanding. Advocate for sustainable choices/lifecycle value in client meetings. Declare conflicts of interest verbally; defend ethical boundaries. Active listening; ensuring quiet voices are heard in discussion. Defend design choices against scrutiny with evidence.
